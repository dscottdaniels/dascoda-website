## Site Architecture Principle
Dascoda should be structured around the positioning: Technology Solutions. Trusted Guidance. Accountable Execution.

Dascoda helps hospitality organizations and multi-site businesses deploy and manage connectivity and guest connectivity solutions through trusted guidance, strategic partner access, and accountable execution.

Dascoda has two lead solution areas:
- Business Internet & Network Services
- Guest Connectivity Solutions

Technology Advisory and Program Leadership are supporting capabilities. They should not be presented as equal primary solution categories.

Approved tagline:
Technology Solutions. Trusted Guidance. Accountable Execution.

Hospitality remains the primary vertical and should lead the messaging hierarchy, but the site architecture must clearly support other multi-site environments where connectivity, mobility, account agreement support, partner coordination, deployment, and execution leadership are relevant.

## Industries Representation
The Industries page should include:
- Hospitality
- Multifamily / MDU
- Commercial Real Estate
- Retail
- Manufacturing
- Other Multi-Site Environments

Hospitality should appear first and receive the strongest emphasis, but the other verticals should be presented as credible, intentional markets rather than afterthoughts.

## Messaging Priorities For Build
- Lead with: Technology Solutions. Trusted Guidance. Accountable Execution.
- Position Dascoda as hospitality-first, not hospitality-only
- Represent hospitality as the primary vertical while clearly supporting Multifamily / MDU, Commercial Real Estate, Retail, Manufacturing, and Other Multi-Site Environments
- Use clearer buyer language: business internet, guest connectivity, network services, partner coordination, and accountable execution
- Keep Passpoint explanations accessible to hotel owners, operators, and executives while presenting Passpoint as part of Guest Connectivity Solutions
- Strengthen Ameriband references while keeping Dascoda clearly independent and client-centered
- Avoid unsupported generic phrases such as "modern guest experiences"

- Hero with the headline "Connectivity, Mobility, and Technology Solutions for Hospitality and Multi-Site Environments"
- Supporting copy about evaluating, sourcing, deploying, and supporting solutions through trusted partners and accountability from strategy through execution
- Industries We Support section covering Hospitality, Multifamily / MDU, Commercial Real Estate, Retail, Manufacturing, and Other Multi-Site Environments

## Guest Connectivity Solutions Page Structure
Use the URL `/guest-connectivity` in Webflow. The current static file remains `mobility-indoor-connectivity.html`.

Do not position the page as only Passpoint, only indoor cellular coverage, or only Ameriband. Passpoint and Ameriband Wi-DAS should remain visible within Guest Connectivity Solutions.

Recommended structure:
- Hero: Guest Connectivity Solutions
- The Business Need: guest connectivity, indoor wireless experience, Wi-Fi Calling, secure onboarding, staff connectivity, solution fit
- Solution Components: Passpoint, Ameriband Wi-DAS, indoor wireless experience, Wi-Fi Calling, other indoor options when needed, partner coordination
- Featured Partner: Ameriband as Dascoda's exclusive hospitality partner for Passpoint-enabled Wi-DAS
- What is Passpoint?
- What is Ameriband Wi-DAS?
- Why Dascoda: needs assessment, solution fit, partner coordination, stakeholder alignment, deployment support, ongoing accountability
- Industries: Hospitality, Multifamily / MDU, Commercial Real Estate, Retail, Manufacturing, Other multi-site environments

Do not change protected Blueprint recommendations for technical SEO, JSON-LD/schema, contact forms, public vs confidential guardrails, tracking, CRM, Guest Connectivity Solutions page structure, or Business Internet page structure.

## Imagery Guidance
Use hospitality imagery as the primary visual language, including hotels, resorts, lobbies, guest-facing property environments, and executive planning moments. Balance that with selective multi-site imagery such as multifamily communities, commercial buildings, retail locations, manufacturing facilities, and portfolio-map visuals.

Avoid making every visual hotel-specific. The design should feel rooted in hospitality but adaptable to property portfolios, campuses, distributed locations, and other operating environments.

## Graphic Motif
Use subtle blue-gradient connectivity lines, nodes, arcs, and location-map patterns to represent service coordination across properties, sites, teams, and partners. Keep these motifs refined and executive rather than technical or telecom-heavy.
