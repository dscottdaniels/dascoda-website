# Dascoda Design System

## Positioning Principle
Dascoda should be presented as Connectivity, Mobility, and Technology Solutions for Hospitality and Multi-Site Environments.

Hospitality is the primary vertical and should lead the story, but the design system must also support Multifamily / MDU, Commercial Real Estate, Retail, Manufacturing, and other multi-site environments where connectivity, mobility, operational continuity, account support, and partner execution matter.

Lead solution areas:
- Business Internet & Network Services
- Mobility & Indoor Connectivity

Supporting capabilities:
- Technology Advisory
- Program Leadership

Technology Advisory and Program Leadership should support the lead solutions visually and structurally. They should not appear as equal top-level solution categories.

## Audience Architecture
Primary audience:
- Hospitality
- Hotel ownership groups
- Hotel management companies
- Hospitality brands
- Resorts

Adjacent multi-site audiences:
- Multifamily / MDU
- Commercial Real Estate
- Retail
- Manufacturing
- Other Multi-Site Environments

The site should speak first to hospitality leaders while making it clear that Dascoda's advisory model applies to other property, portfolio, and multi-location operating environments.

## Navigation Guidance
Keep the main navigation simple and executive:
- Solutions
- Industries
- Mobility & Indoor Connectivity
- Partners
- About
- Contact

The Industries page should represent the full market set, not only hotels and resorts. Hospitality may be visually prioritized, but adjacent verticals should appear as credible, first-class use cases.

## Industry Representation
Industry cards, icons, and supporting visuals should include:
- Hospitality: hotels, resorts, ownership groups, management companies, brands
- Multifamily / MDU: resident connectivity, property portfolios, managed communities
- Commercial Real Estate: office, mixed-use, tenant experience, building connectivity
- Retail: multi-location stores, customer access, staff mobility, operational consistency
- Manufacturing: facilities, workforce mobility, industrial campus connectivity
- Other Multi-Site Environments: distributed operations with repeatable service needs

Avoid language or layouts that imply Dascoda only serves hotels.

## Visual System
Use the approved Dascoda logo palette:
- Dascoda Navy: `#1F3A5F`
- Dascoda Blue Gradient: `#2F5FA8` to `#5F8FD6`
- Dascoda Red: `#C63C32`
- Dascoda Green: `#4F8F4A`
- Slate Gray: `#6B7280`
- Light Background Gray: `#F5F7FA`
- White: `#FFFFFF`

Use navy for brand presence, navigation, headers, and footer. Use the blue gradient for connectivity graphics and section accents. Use red sparingly for primary calls to action. Use green only for positive/supporting accents.

## Imagery Guidance
Imagery should feel premium, operational, and relationship-led.

Use hospitality imagery as the primary visual language:
- Hotel lobbies
- Resorts
- Meeting and conference spaces
- Guest-facing property environments
- Ownership or management team planning moments

Add adjacent multi-site imagery where appropriate:
- Multifamily properties and managed communities
- Commercial buildings and mixed-use properties
- Retail locations and store operations
- Manufacturing facilities and campus environments
- Portfolio maps or multi-location operating visuals

Avoid imagery that feels like generic telecom infrastructure, server rooms, stock call centers, or abstract technology marketing.

## Design Direction
The visual design should communicate:
- Executive credibility
- Hospitality expertise
- Multi-site operational experience
- Technology sophistication
- Long-term partnership

Avoid:
- Looking like a telecom reseller
- Looking like a generic consulting company
- Looking hospitality-only
- Looking like an Ameriband sales page

Use hospitality as the lead story, but show enough property, portfolio, and multi-site context to make Dascoda's broader relevance clear.

## Graphic Motif
Use subtle connectivity lines, nodes, arcs, and portfolio-map patterns as supporting graphics. These should feel architectural and executive, not overly technical. The motif can connect hospitality and multi-site environments by suggesting coordination across properties, locations, teams, and partners.

## Content Hierarchy
Recommended page-level framing:
- Lead with Connectivity, Mobility, and Technology Solutions for Hospitality and Multi-Site Environments
- Present Passpoint as a technology within Mobility & Indoor Connectivity, not as the page or solution category
- Clarify that hospitality is the primary vertical, not the only vertical
- Emphasize business outcomes: service continuity, account alignment, deployment coordination, stakeholder clarity, and execution support
- Use industry examples to broaden relevance without diluting the hospitality core
