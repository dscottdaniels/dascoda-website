# Visual Direction

## Brand Feel
Dascoda should feel professional, executive, hospitality-first, multi-site aware, and quietly confident. The visual system should communicate trusted solutions guidance and execution accountability, not software complexity, generic consulting, or telecom reseller energy.

## Design Attributes
- Executive
- Calm
- Precise
- Hospitality-aware
- Multi-site aware
- Partner-oriented
- Modern but not trendy
- Warm enough for relationship-led advisory
- Technologically sophisticated without feeling overly technical

## Color Direction
Use the approved Dascoda logo palette as the source of truth:
- Dascoda Navy: `#1F3A5F` for primary headers, navigation, footer, and executive brand presence
- Dascoda Blue Gradient: `#2F5FA8` to `#5F8FD6` for hero accents, subtle section backgrounds, and connectivity line or node graphics
- Dascoda Red: `#C63C32` sparingly for primary CTAs or important emphasis
- Dascoda Green: `#4F8F4A` only for positive/supporting accents, never as the main brand color
- Slate Gray: `#6B7280` for body text and subtext
- Light Background Gray: `#F5F7FA` for alternating sections and quiet content bands
- White: `#FFFFFF` for the main page background, cards, and high-clarity content areas
- Avoid random teal, copper, neon blue, and heavy technical black backgrounds unless specifically approved

## Typography Direction
- Use a polished sans-serif family with strong readability
- Headings should feel confident and editorial
- Body copy should be clear, spacious, and executive-friendly
- Avoid overly futuristic or technical typefaces

## Imagery Direction
- Hospitality environments: lobbies, guest spaces, resorts, meeting areas, managed properties
- Multi-site environments: multifamily communities, commercial real estate, retail locations, manufacturing facilities, property portfolios
- Subtle connectivity cues: devices, property technology, wireless coverage, operational teams
- Executive advisory moments: leadership meetings, planning, partner collaboration
- Avoid stock photos that feel like generic call centers, server rooms, or abstract network graphics
- Avoid making the full site feel hospitality-only or like an Ameriband sales page

## UI Direction
- Clean sections with generous spacing
- Compact capability grids
- Subtle borders and neutral backgrounds
- Minimal animation
- Clear CTAs
- Use partner and process visuals sparingly

## Webflow Component Mood
- Header should feel stable and simple
- CTA bands should be concise
- Cards should be restrained and content-led
- Forms should be clean, direct, and easy to complete
